 <task definition>
    You are predicting the entry station of a passenger's next metro trip.
    Using the passenger's historical travel behavior, recent travel context,
    and long-term mobility persona, infer the single most probable
    entry station for the target trip.
    ________________________________________
    <persona>
    <persona> describes the passenger's long-term mobility characteristics
    as a 7-element tuple:
    (Total_Trips, Active_Days, Unique_Origin, Unique_Dests, Avg_TT,
    Peak_Trips, Weekend_Trips)
    - Total_Trips: total trips completed during the observation period.
    - Active_Days: number of distinct days with at least one trip.
    - Unique_Origin: number of unique boarding stations used.
    - Unique_Dests: number of unique destination stations visited.
    - Avg_TT: average travel time across all trips.
    - Peak_Trips: number of trips made during peak commuting periods.
    - Weekend_Trips: number of trips made on weekends.
    ________________________________________
    <data description>
    <history> contains the passenger's older trips; <context> contains
    their most recent trips immediately before the prediction target.
    Both are in chronological order. Each trip is a 7-element tuple:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, Entry_DT, Exit_DT, DayName,
    Entry_Station_Name, Exit_Station_Name)
    The prediction target <target_stay> has the same format with the exit
    station hidden:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, Entry_DT, None, DayName,
    <entry_station_name>, None)
    Only <entry_station_name> must be predicted.
    ________________________________________
    <thinking guidance>
    Reason step by step before answering:
    1. Scan <history> for repeated origin-destination pairs tied to this
       Exit_Station_Name, DayName, and time of day.
    2. Check <context> for the most recent trend.
    3. Match the target's demographics and temporal attributes against
       when similar destinations occurred in the past.
    4. Weigh <persona> -- high Unique_Dests means more varied behavior.
    5. Combine into one most-likely entry station.
    Output only the final prediction in the required JSON format.
