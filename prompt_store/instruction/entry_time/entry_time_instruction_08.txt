Task title: Instruction 8

<task definition>
    Forecast the next bike ride time of a cyclist based on their historical biking patterns, recent biking context, and personal biking habits.
    ________________________________________
    <persona>
    <persona> captures the cyclist's biking habits as a 7-element tuple:
    (Total_Bikes, Active_Days, Unique_Riding_Paths, Favorite_Paths, Avg_Bike_Time,
    Peak_Biking_Hours, Weekend_Bikes)
    - Total_Bikes: total number of bike rides recorded.
    - Active_Days: number of distinct days with at least one bike ride.
    - Unique_Riding_Paths: number of distinct biking paths used.
    - Favorite_Paths: top 3 most frequently biked paths.
    - Avg_Bike_Time: average duration of each bike ride.
    - Peak_Biking_Hours: hours with the highest biking frequency.
    - Weekend_Bikes: number of rides taken on weekends.
    ________________________________________
    <data description>
    <history> lists past biking records; <context> shows recent rides leading up to the prediction target. Both are in chronological order. Each ride is a 7-element tuple:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, Start_DT, End_DT, DayName, Starting_Point, Ending_Point)
    The prediction target <target_bike> has the same format but with the ending point hidden:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, <start_time>, None, DayName, Starting_Point, None)
    Only <start_time> must be predicted.
    ________________________________________
    <thinking guidance>
    Use the following method to predict the next bike ride start time:
    1. Examine <history> for repeated starting and ending points matching the <target_bike>.
    2. Analyze <context> for the most recent biking trends.
    3. Consider the user's demographic information.
    4. Factor in the average bike ride time and peak biking hours.
    5. Combine all data to form a reasoned prediction.
    Provide the result in the required JSON format.
