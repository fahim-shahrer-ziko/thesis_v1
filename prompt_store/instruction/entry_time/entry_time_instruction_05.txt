Task title: Instruction 5

<task definition>
    Determine the entry time of a subway rider's next train ride using their past travel patterns, current travel context, and personal mobility habits.
    ________________________________________
    <persona>
    <persona> captures the subway rider's travel habits as a 7-element tuple:
    (Total_Trains, Active_Days, Unique_Stations, Favorite_Stations, Avg_Ride_Time,
    Peak_Travel_Hours, Weekend_Rides)
    - Total_Trains: total number of trains taken over the observed period.
    - Active_Days: number of distinct days with at least one train ride.
    - Unique_Stations: number of distinct subway stations used.
    - Favorite_Stations: top 3 most frequently boarded stations.
    - Avg_Ride_Time: average time spent on each train ride.
    - Peak_Travel_Hours: hours with the highest train ride frequency.
    - Weekend_Rides: number of rides taken on weekends.
    ________________________________________
    <data description>
    <history> includes past train rides; <context> shows recent rides leading up to the prediction target. Both are in chronological order. Each ride is a 7-element tuple:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, Entry_DT, Exit_DT, DayName, Boarding_Station, Exiting_Station)
    The prediction target <target_train> has the same format but with the exiting station hidden:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, <entry_time>, None, DayName, Boarding_Station, None)
    Only <entry_time> must be predicted.
    ________________________________________
    <thinking guidance>
    Follow these steps to predict the next train entry time:
    1. Examine <history> for repeated boarding and exiting stations matching the <target_train>.
    2. Analyze <context> for the most recent travel trends.
    3. Consider the user's demographic information.
    4. Factor in the average ride time and peak travel hours.
    5. Combine all data to form a reasoned prediction.
    Provide the result in the required JSON format.
