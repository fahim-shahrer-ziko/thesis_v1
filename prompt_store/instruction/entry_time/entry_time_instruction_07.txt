Task title: Instruction 7

<task definition>
    Predict the next walking route of a hiker based on their past hiking activities, recent hiking context, and personal hiking habits.
    ________________________________________
    <persona>
    <persona> describes the hiker's hiking habits as a 7-element tuple:
    (Total_Hikes, Active_Days, Unique_Hiking_Paths, Favorite_Paths, Avg_Hike_Duration,
    Peak_Hiking_Hours, Weekend_Hikes)
    - Total_Hikes: total number of hikes recorded.
    - Active_Days: number of distinct days with at least one hike.
    - Unique_Hiking_Paths: number of distinct hiking paths used.
    - Favorite_Paths: top 3 most frequently hiked paths.
    - Avg_Hike_Duration: average duration of each hike.
    - Peak_Hiking_Hours: hours with the highest hiking frequency.
    - Weekend_Hikes: number of hikes taken on weekends.
    ________________________________________
    <data description>
    <history> includes past hiking records; <context> shows recent hikes leading up to the prediction target. Both are in chronological order. Each hike is a 7-element tuple:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, Start_DT, End_DT, DayName, Starting_Point, Ending_Point)
    The prediction target <target_hike> has the same format but with the ending point hidden:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, <start_time>, None, DayName, Starting_Point, None)
    Only <start_time> must be predicted.
    ________________________________________
    <thinking guidance>
    Follow these steps to predict the next hiking start time:
    1. Review <history> for repeated starting and ending points matching the <target_hike>.
    2. Analyze <context> for the most recent hiking trends.
    3. Consider the user's demographic information.
    4. Factor in the average hike duration and peak hiking hours.
    5. Combine all data to form a reasoned prediction.
    Provide the result in the required JSON format.
