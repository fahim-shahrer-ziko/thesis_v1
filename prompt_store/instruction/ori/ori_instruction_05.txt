Task title: Instruction 5

<task definition>
    Forecast the exit station for a weekend commuter based on their historical weekend trip patterns and current context.
    ________________________________________
    <persona>
    <persona> describes the commuter's long-term mobility characteristics
    as a 7-element tuple:
    (Total_Trips, Active_Days, Unique_Origin, Unique_Dests, Avg_TT,
    Peak_Trips, Weekend_Trips)
    - Total_Trips: total trips completed during the observation period.
    - Active_Days: number of distinct days with at least one trip.
    - Unique_Origin: number of unique starting stations used.
    - Unique_Dests: number of unique destination stations visited.
    - Avg_TT: average travel time across all trips.
    - Peak_Trips: number of trips made during peak commuting periods.
    - Weekend_Trips: number of trips made on weekends.
    ________________________________________
    <data description>
    <history> contains the commuter's older weekend trips; <context> contains
    their most recent weekend trips immediately before the prediction target.
    Both are in chronological order. Each trip is a 7-element tuple:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, Entry_DT, Exit_DT, DayName,
    Entry_Station_Name, Exit_Station_Name)
    The prediction target <target_stay> has the same format with the exit station hidden:
    (Gender, Age_On_June_2024, Entry_DT, None, DayName,
    <entry_station_name>, None)
    Only <entry_station_name> must be predicted.
    ________________________________________
    <thinking guidance>
    Reason step by step before answering:
    1. Identify common exit points from <history>.
    2. Analyze <context> for recent patterns.
    3. Consider the commuter's age and the day of the week for the target trip.
    4. Use <persona> to understand typical weekend travel behavior.
    5. Synthesize the information to make a final prediction.
    Output only the final prediction in the required JSON format.
